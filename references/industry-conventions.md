# Industry Conventions

Visual patterns, typography approaches, and cliches by industry. Use this during thesis writing and when running persona perspectives to ground decisions in domain context.

---

## Fintech

**Common visual patterns:** Gradient backgrounds, abstract geometric shapes, dashboard screenshots, currency symbols as visual elements, card-shaped UI components
**Dominant typography:** Clean geometric sans-serifs (Circular, Graphik, GT Walsheim). Heavy use of monospace for numbers and data
**Cliches to avoid:** Navy and gold palettes, stock photos of handshakes, "trusted by millions" messaging, copying Stripe's aesthetic wholesale
**Emerging directions:** Warmer color palettes that signal accessibility over exclusivity, illustration-forward approaches (Wise, Cash App), bold brand characters, departure from "we look like a bank" toward "we look like your friend who happens to handle money"

---

## Healthcare

**Common visual patterns:** Clean white space, soft rounded shapes, medical photography, trust badges and certifications, calming gradients
**Dominant typography:** Humanist sans-serifs (Freight Sans, Brandon Grotesque). Rounded fonts to feel approachable. Serif for authority
**Cliches to avoid:** Teal and white everything, stock photos of smiling doctors, heart/cross iconography as logos, clinical sterility passed off as design
**Emerging directions:** Mental health destigmatization driving bolder aesthetics (Headspace, Calm), illustration replacing photography, warmer and more human color palettes, conversational brand voice

---

## Luxury

**Common visual patterns:** Full-bleed photography, minimal text, generous white space, monogram patterns, editorial layouts borrowed from fashion magazines
**Dominant typography:** High-contrast serif fonts (Didot, Bodoni), ultra-thin sans-serifs, custom typefaces as brand assets
**Cliches to avoid:** Black and gold default, marble textures, "heritage since [year]" messaging, script fonts for "elegance," looking like every other luxury brand
**Emerging directions:** Quiet luxury aesthetics (less logo, more texture), digital-native luxury (Ssense, Farfetch), sustainability as luxury positioning, brutalist web design in high-end spaces

---

## DTC / E-commerce

**Common visual patterns:** Lifestyle photography, unboxing moments, Instagram-ready product shots, pastel palettes, playful packaging as marketing
**Dominant typography:** Friendly geometric sans-serifs, mix of sans-serif and rounded serif. Display fonts for personality
**Cliches to avoid:** Millennial pink, generic "we're different" messaging, identical Shopify template aesthetics, copying Glossier/Away/Casper
**Emerging directions:** Maximalist design pushing back against millennial minimalism, 3D product visualization, community-driven visual identity, return to bold illustration and hand-lettering

---

## B2B SaaS

**Common visual patterns:** Product screenshots, abstract 3D shapes, isometric illustrations, gradient meshes, dashboard UI as hero imagery
**Dominant typography:** System-friendly sans-serifs, geometric fonts that render well on screen, increasing use of variable fonts
**Cliches to avoid:** Blue. So much blue. Purple gradients. Abstract blob illustrations that say nothing. "Platform for [category]" messaging
**Emerging directions:** Personality-driven B2B brands (Linear, Notion, Figma), illustration styles with actual character, dark mode as primary presentation, editorial content as brand differentiator

---

## Food & Beverage

**Common visual patterns:** Close-up food photography, ingredient textures, craft/artisanal packaging, hand-drawn elements, farm-to-table imagery
**Dominant typography:** Hand-lettered or script for craft positioning, bold slab serifs for mass market, clean sans-serif for health positioning
**Cliches to avoid:** Red and yellow for appetite stimulation, rustic kraft paper for "artisanal," green for healthy, overly styled food photography that looks fake
**Emerging directions:** Bold geometric packaging (Oatly, Liquid Death), irreverent brand voice, sustainability transparency in visual design, functional food brands borrowing from tech aesthetics

---

## Sustainability / Climate Tech

**Common visual patterns:** Nature photography, earth tones, leaf/tree iconography, data visualization of environmental impact, certification badges
**Dominant typography:** Rounded humanist sans-serifs, organic letterforms, eco-coded fonts with soft edges
**Cliches to avoid:** Green palette (any shade), leaf logos, earth imagery as a crutch, looking so earnest that you become invisible next to competitors doing the same thing
**Emerging directions:** Urgency-driven design (bold colors, stark messaging), tech-forward aesthetics that signal innovation not sentimentality, dark backgrounds with electric accents, data-driven visual storytelling

---

## Creator Economy

**Common visual patterns:** Gradient backgrounds, neon accents, creator headshots, social proof metrics, platform screenshots, motion-heavy design
**Dominant typography:** Display fonts with personality, variable-width fonts, custom type treatments, streetwear-influenced lettering
**Cliches to avoid:** Purple and pink gradients (Twitch/Instagram coding), rocket ship imagery, "10x your growth" messaging, looking like every other creator tool
**Emerging directions:** Anti-corporate aesthetics, brutalist interfaces, community-generated visual content, personal branding systems that scale, AI-native design patterns

---

## Travel & Hospitality

**Common visual patterns:** Full-bleed destination photography, map elements, booking UI patterns, local cultural motifs, seasonal campaigns
**Dominant typography:** Elegant serifs for luxury travel, friendly sans-serifs for budget, display fonts for adventure positioning
**Cliches to avoid:** Postcard photography, generic "wanderlust" messaging, compass/globe logos, blue for ocean/sky, looking like an airline when you're not
**Emerging directions:** Experience-forward design over destination photography (Airbnb), editorial travel content, local culture integration, sustainable travel as design narrative

---

## Gaming

**Common visual patterns:** Dark backgrounds, neon and electric colors, angular shapes, character-driven design, immersive worlds, HUD-influenced UI
**Dominant typography:** Custom display fonts, sci-fi influenced type, angular geometric fonts, variable fonts for responsive experiences
**Cliches to avoid:** Red and black default, aggressive angular everything, generic sci-fi aesthetics, "epic" and "legendary" in every piece of copy
**Emerging directions:** Cozy gaming aesthetics (softer palettes, rounded shapes), cross-cultural design for global audiences, community identity systems, physical merchandise as brand extension
