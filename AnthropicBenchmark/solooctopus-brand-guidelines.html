01 — Brand Identity

The story behind the name

Solo: One person with the vision, taste, and relationships to build something extraordinary. Octopus: Eight arms — each an AI agent handling editing, distribution, social, SEO, scheduling, analytics, design, and outreach — simultaneously. Intelligent. Adaptable. Everywhere at once.

Mission
Democratize world-class media production
Give celebrities a full-service studio experience without the 30-person production team. AI agents handle the execution. The human brings the magic.
Vision
Every voice deserves infrastructure
The future of media is personal. Stars shouldn't need conglomerates to sound legendary. SoloOctopus is the studio in your pocket — and the team around the clock.
Values
Precision. Velocity. Authenticity.
We move fast without sacrificing quality. We use AI to augment taste, not replace it. We believe the best content is honest, specific, and beautifully packaged.

05 — Voice & Tone

How we speak

SoloOctopus speaks with the confidence of someone who has already shipped. We don't pitch. We demonstrate. We're precise, culturally fluent, and never corporate.

Pillar 01
Precise, not technical
We know the technology deeply but we don't lead with it. We translate AI capability into creative outcomes. "Your episode drops Thursday" — not "our NLP pipeline processed your audio."
Pillar 02
Confident, not arrogant
We know what we're building is remarkable. We state it plainly without overselling. Our track record speaks. We let work do the talking.
Pillar 03
Warm, not casual
We work with celebrities who are used to being handled. We treat them as creative partners. Professional without stiffness. Friendly without fandom.

Voice in action

✓ SoloOctopus
"Your last episode hit 2.1M impressions in 48 hours. Here's what we're doing differently this week."
Specific. Results-forward. Implies ongoing optimization without jargon.
✗ Avoid
"We leverage cutting-edge AI-powered content optimization algorithms to maximize your digital media footprint."
Corporate, vague, buzzword-heavy. Sounds like every agency pitch deck.
✓ SoloOctopus
"We handled the edit, the clips, the captions, the scheduling. You recorded. That's how it should work."
Clear value prop. Emphasizes what the client doesn't have to do. Effortless.
✗ Avoid
"Our comprehensive content management solution streamlines your multimedia production workflow."
No human. No warmth. Could be a SaaS product selling to anyone.
✓ SoloOctopus
"One person. Eight AI arms. No production crew required."
Punchy, memorable, on-brand. Uses our own metaphor to explain our model.
✗ Avoid
"We're a small but mighty team disrupting the traditional media production industry!"
Startup clichés. "Disrupting" is overused. "Small but mighty" sounds apologetic.

Tone by context

Context Tone Example
Client onboarding Warm, structured, reassuring "We've done this before. Here's exactly what happens next."
Social media (brand) Confident, punchy, slightly irreverent "While your old podcast editor was sleeping, our agents shipped 14 clips."
Website copy Precise, editorial, premium "A studio without walls. A team without limits."
Episode descriptions Energetic, culturally aware, specific "She's sold out arenas. Now she's talking about the deal she almost didn't take."
Client reports Direct, data-driven, solution-focused "Views dropped 12% this week. We've adjusted the thumbnail format and posting window."
Error / problem states Calm, accountable, action-oriented "Something went sideways. We caught it. Here's what we're doing."

07 — Motion & Animation

How things move

Motion communicates intelligence. Transitions are fast and purposeful — like an AI agent executing. No gratuitous animation. Every motion has a reason.

Easing
cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)
Our signature ease. Fast in, gentle settle. Like a decision being made with confidence.
Duration — Fast
150ms
Hover states, button feedback, icon changes. Instant feel.
Duration — Base
250ms
Card reveals, dropdown open, modal entrance. Standard.
Duration — Slow
400ms
Page transitions, hero entrances, loading sequences. Cinematic moments.
Scroll Animation
fadeUp + 20px
Elements enter from slightly below. Stagger: 100ms between siblings.
Hover Lift
translateY(-2px)
Cards and buttons lift subtly on hover. Never more than 2–4px.
Principles
Do use motion for
✓ State changes (hover, active, focus)
✓ Content entering the viewport
✓ Loading and processing feedback
✓ Confirming user actions
✓ Navigating between views
Anti-patterns
Avoid motion for
✗ Decorative or attention-seeking animation
✗ Loops that continue without interaction
✗ Transitions longer than 500ms in UI
✗ Animations that delay access to content
✗ Any motion that can't be disabled (respect prefers-reduced-motion)

12 — The Non-Negotiables

Brand laws

🐙
Always dark-first

SoloOctopus lives in the dark. Our primary surfaces are Ink and Ink Soft. Light mode is a secondary consideration, never the default.

🐙
Purple owns the accent

Electric Purple (#6E3FF3) is our primary accent. It is never used for decorative purposes — only for interactive elements, the logo, and key highlights.

🐙
One CTA per view

Stripe's greatest lesson: reduce friction by focusing attention. Every screen, slide, or email has one primary action. Everything else is secondary.

🐙
Inter for UI, Playfair for impact

Never use Playfair Display for body copy. Never use Inter for hero moments that need editorial weight. Each font has its domain.

⚠️
Never use gradients on text

Gradient text is a 2015 design trend. Our text is always solid. Gradients live on backgrounds, shapes, and the logo mark only.

⚠️
Never say "AI-powered" in client-facing copy

It's table stakes. Instead: describe what agents actually do. "Blade cut 14 clips" is more powerful than "AI-powered content creation."

⚠️
Never compete on price

We price for the outcome, not the hours. Red Set Media charges more. We charge appropriately. Never lead with cost savings as our primary value proposition.

⚠️
Never look like a startup

No neon, no pixel art, no Discord-era vibes. We serve celebrities who've worked with the best. Our brand must feel like it belongs in that room.
