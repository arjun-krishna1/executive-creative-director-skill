# Photography Vocabulary

Shared vocabulary for specific, actionable photography directions. Replaces vague mood descriptions with controllable dimensions.

## Photography Styles

| Style | Definition | Brand Signal | Real Example | Persona Fit |
|-------|-----------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| Editorial | Curated, magazine-quality compositions. Every element placed with intent. Subjects styled, environments controlled | Considered taste. Brand as curator | Cereal Magazine, COS campaigns | Bierut |
| Documentary | Unscripted moments captured in real environments. Story through observation, not staging | Authenticity. Brand as witness | Patagonia "Worn Wear," Carhartt WIP lookbooks | Scher |
| Lifestyle | Aspirational but attainable scenes. Products woven into idealized daily life | Belonging. Brand as world you enter | Glossier "Skin First," Muji catalog photography | Collins |
| Candid | Raw, often unexpected frames. Imperfect focus, motion blur, genuine reaction | Energy. Brand as captured moment | Supreme box logo campaigns, Martin Parr for Gucci | Scher |
| Studio | Controlled environment, clean backgrounds. Product as hero with precise lighting | Precision. Brand as engineered object | Apple product photography, Braun catalog shots | Bierut |
| Environmental | People and products in their natural habitat. Context tells the story | Community. Brand as shared space | Early WeWork, Kinfolk magazine features | Collins |

## Mood Vocabulary

Build mood from three dimensions. Every photography direction must name at least one term from each.

### Lighting

| Term | Visual Effect | Emotional Signal |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Golden hour | Warm, directional, long shadows | Nostalgia, warmth, human scale |
| Overcast flat | Even, shadowless, muted tones | Calm, Nordic, understated |
| Harsh noon | High contrast, short shadows, bleached | Raw energy, unfiltered truth |
| Studio controlled | Even key light, shaped shadows, precision | Authority, product clarity |
| Natural window | Soft directional, interior warmth | Intimacy, domesticity, real life |
| Neon/artificial | Colored cast, urban glow, saturated | Youth culture, nightlife, edge |
| Dappled | Broken light through foliage/blinds, organic patterns | Organic, transient, poetic |
| Backlit silhouette | Subject dark against bright background, rim light | Drama, anonymity, scale |

### Composition

| Term | Visual Effect | Emotional Signal |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Tight crop | Subject fills frame, minimal context | Intensity, focus, confrontation |
| Environmental wide | Subject small in setting, context dominant | Story, place, belonging |
| Negative space dominant | 60%+ empty area, subject isolated | Confidence, breathing room, luxury |
| Layered depth | Foreground/midground/background all active | Complexity, richness, discovery |
| Symmetrical | Center-weighted, balanced, formal | Order, authority, intentionality |
| Dutch angle | Tilted horizon, dynamic tension | Disruption, energy, unease |
| Overhead flat-lay | Bird's eye, objects arranged on surface | Curation, detail, editorial |
| Eye-level intimate | Camera at subject height, direct gaze | Connection, equality, honesty |

### Post-Processing

| Term | Visual Effect | Emotional Signal |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Film grain | Visible texture, analog warmth, imperfection | Heritage, craft, human touch |
| Desaturated matte | Pulled-back color, flat shadows, no blacks | Restraint, sophistication, quiet |
| High contrast | Deep blacks, bright whites, punchy midtones | Drama, boldness, impact |
| Color-graded cinematic | Shifted color temperature, teal-orange, mood control | Narrative, aspiration, world-building |
| Unprocessed raw | Minimal editing, true-to-life color, full tonal range | Honesty, transparency, confidence |
| Duotone | Image mapped to two brand colors | Graphic identity, recognition, system |
| Cross-processed | Shifted hues, unexpected color cast, retro | Rebellion, nostalgia, subcultural |

## Subject Direction

Specific pairs that force choices rather than leaving direction open.

| Option A | Option B | What the choice reveals |
|----------|----------|------------------------|
| People in motion | People at rest | Energy vs. contemplation |
| Products in context | Products isolated | Story vs. object worship |
| Environments with people | Environments empty | Community vs. aspiration |
| Details / textures | Wide establishing shots | Craft vs. scale |
| Hands / process | Faces / portraits | Making vs. being |
| Behind-the-scenes | Final result | Transparency vs. polish |
| Groups / community | Individuals | Belonging vs. independence |
| Urban settings | Natural settings | Culture vs. nature |
| Styled / curated | Found / unplanned | Control vs. spontaneity |
| High angle / power | Low angle / vulnerability | Authority vs. intimacy |

## Real Brand Photography Briefs

One-line descriptions that prove specificity is possible.

- **Aesop** -- Studio-lit product on textured stone, desaturated matte, negative space dominant. No people, no lifestyle. The formulation is the story
- **Patagonia** -- Documentary wide shots of athletes in extreme terrain, unprocessed raw, environmental composition. The mountain is bigger than the jacket
- **Apple** -- Studio controlled on seamless white/black, tight crop on material details, high contrast. Product as sculpture
- **Acne Studios** -- Candid portraits with harsh noon or neon lighting, tight crop, film grain. Fashion as attitude, not aspiration
- **Nike** -- Backlit silhouette athletes in motion, high contrast, layered depth. The body is the product
- **Rapha** -- Documentary cycling in golden hour landscapes, desaturated matte, environmental wide. Suffering as beauty
- **Muji** -- Overhead flat-lay of objects on natural materials, overcast flat lighting, unprocessed raw. Absence as design
- **Glossier** -- Natural window light on real skin, eye-level intimate, unprocessed raw. The person is the product, not the product
- **Bottega Veneta** -- Studio controlled on solid color backgrounds, negative space dominant, color-graded cinematic. Leather whispers, never shouts
- **Kinfolk** -- Environmental wide in domestic spaces, natural window light, desaturated matte. The quiet life, elevated
- **Arc'teryx** -- Documentary in alpine conditions, harsh noon or overcast flat, environmental wide. Technical gear in technical terrain
- **Comme des Garcons** -- Dutch angle candid, neon/artificial light, high contrast. Fashion as provocation
- **Hay Design** -- Studio controlled with overcast flat light, symmetrical composition, desaturated matte. Objects in conversation with each other
- **The North Face** -- Backlit silhouette on summit ridges, golden hour, environmental wide. Scale of ambition vs. scale of human
- **Diptyque** -- Studio controlled with dappled light, layered depth (candle + environment), film grain. Scent made visible

## Persona Photography Tendencies

Natural gravitational pull for each persona. Starting positions, not mandates.

### Bierut (The Servant)
- **Styles:** Editorial, Studio
- **Lighting:** Studio controlled, Overcast flat
- **Composition:** Symmetrical, Negative space dominant
- **Post-processing:** Desaturated matte, Unprocessed raw
- **Philosophy:** Photography as repeatable system. Every shot should feel like it belongs to the same grid. Consistency over expression. The brand identity carries the emotion -- the photography is the stage, not the performer

### Scher (The Expressionist)
- **Styles:** Documentary, Candid
- **Lighting:** Harsh noon, Neon/artificial
- **Composition:** Tight crop, Dutch angle
- **Post-processing:** High contrast, Cross-processed
- **Philosophy:** Photography as caught energy. The best brand photo looks like an accident that reveals truth. Imperfection is information. Blur, grain, and tilt are tools, not mistakes

### Collins (The Futurist)
- **Styles:** Lifestyle, Environmental
- **Lighting:** Golden hour, Natural window
- **Composition:** Environmental wide, Layered depth
- **Post-processing:** Color-graded cinematic, Film grain
- **Philosophy:** Photography as world-building. Every image should make you want to step inside it. The brand creates a universe -- photography is the portal. Mood is strategy, not decoration

## Anti-Patterns

Vague directions and their fixes. If you write anything in the left column, rewrite using vocabulary from this file.

| Vague Direction | What's Missing | Fix |
|----------------|---------------|-----|
| "Editorial photography" | Which style? What lighting? What composition? | Name the style + at least one term from each mood dimension |
| "Warm tones" | Warm how? Golden hour warmth or post-processed warmth? | Specify: golden hour lighting + film grain, or color-graded cinematic with warm shift |
| "Clean and modern" | Meaningless without specifics | Studio controlled + symmetrical + desaturated matte = the specific version of "clean" |
| "Lifestyle photography" | Lifestyle is a style, not a direction | Lifestyle + golden hour + environmental wide + color-graded cinematic = a direction |
| "Bold and dynamic" | Every brand wants this. Says nothing | Candid + harsh noon + tight crop + high contrast = the specific version of "bold" |
| "Professional quality" | Not a creative direction. Just a production standard | Delete this entirely. Quality is assumed. Direct the mood instead |
| "Natural and authentic" | Could mean documentary or lifestyle or candid | Documentary + overcast flat + eye-level intimate + unprocessed raw = specificity |
| "Aspirational" | Aspiration to what? For whom? | Environmental + golden hour + layered depth + color-graded cinematic = aspiration made specific |
